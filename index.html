Welcome to our work, creating a link between biotechnological invention and chemical know-how!

PGM-Bioleaching

With these two disciplines combined, we present a new, efficient and far cheaper method to re-harvest platin-like elements from the remnants of the catalysts. We have modified bacteria to isolate platin-like elements from these remnants and to re-harvest those elements by established chemical purification methods. Interlinked with these experiments, we have created own models and assays to optimize our analysis and to elevate our working cycle's efficiency constantly. Furthermore we have endeavoured to share our compassion for our project and for biology in general with the world and have passed on interest in these fields to various groups of people. We invite you to learn more about our work, our results and is on this website ! Enjoy :)

Project Overview

In times of resource scarcity, global warming and overexploitation, it is our duty to find methods of responsible recycling. This was our mission with this project. We approach recycling of precious platin group metals (PGMs) used in catalysators with bioleaching using C. glutamicum.

Bioleaching advantages
